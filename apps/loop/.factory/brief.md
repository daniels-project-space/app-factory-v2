# Loop — Three daily anchors, a handful of tiny habits, and a streak flame you'd hate to let shrink.

## Positioning
For people who've abandoned five habit apps because 20-item checklists feel like a second job. The pain isn't tracking — it's the all-or-nothing guilt that makes one missed day feel like total failure, so they quit. Loop wins by shrinking the surface area to three moments a day (morning/midday/evening), 1–3 tiny habits each, and a flame that *shrinks instead of resets* when you slip — so a bad day costs you a little, not everything. That single loss-aversion mechanic reframes consistency from perfection to protection, which is the psychological unlock every streak app fumbles. The weekly reflection card turns private effort into a shareable artifact, giving organic top-of-funnel that competitors buy with ads.

## Persona
Maya, 29, a designer who starts every January with grand routines and a fresh habit app, then bails by February when the checklist balloons and the streak snaps. She doesn't want a life-optimization dashboard — she wants three gentle nudges a day and a reason not to break the chain that doesn't punish her into quitting.

## Pricing
$4.99/week or $39.99/year (33% implied discount), hard paywall triggered on the 3rd anchor completion or first weekly reflection — whichever comes first. Free tier: 1 anchor, up to 2 habits, current-week reflection view only.

## MVP features
- Three fixed anchors (morning/midday/evening) each holding 1–3 tiny habits, with a single-tap complete gesture per habit
- The signature living flame: a physically-simulated ember that grows with completed anchors and visibly shrinks (never resets) on a missed day
- Anchor-time notifications that fire at the user's set windows with in-voice copy ('midday check — two small things')
- Weekly reflection screen that composes the week into a shareable, export-ready story card (image + link)
- Paywall moment wired to the loss-aversion beat: unlock unlimited habits, custom anchor times, and the reflection archive
- Streak state that persists offline and reconciles on reopen so the flame is always truthful
- Onboarding that has you pick exactly 3 tiny habits in under 60 seconds — no blank-slate paralysis
- Light + dark themes driven from shared design tokens, flame palette tuned for both

## Brand
warm ember, dark hearth, quiet resolve — primary #E8552B, accent #F2B441, font Sora

## Risks
- Habit trackers are a retention graveyard — D30 is brutal and the flame novelty may not survive the first genuinely bad week; needs a re-engagement beat beyond notifications
- Weekly pricing on a category users expect for free or one-time ($5 Streaks) invites sticker shock and refund/chargeback risk — annual must carry the revenue
- Loss-aversion can backfire: a shrinking flame may read as nagging/shame and accelerate churn for the exact anxious users it targets — needs careful tuning and an off-ramp tone
- Three-anchor rigidity is the wedge but also the ceiling — power users will feel boxed in and the TAM is narrower than a general tracker
- Shareable card virality is unproven; if reflection cards don't get posted, CAC stays paid and unit economics get thin against a crowded ad market
